Welcome!

I am currently a student at Brigham Young University seeking a degree in Information Systems. I always have had a passion for problem-solving and creating new ways to overcome obstacles. Throughout my education, this love for problem-solving has transferred to my love for coding, learning new programming languages, and exploring a career in Information Systems. Developing skills like critical thinking has been fulfilling in my education thus far, and I look forward to continuing to expand my abilities.

The purpose of this website is to showcase some of the skills that I have developed and display projects that I have created. This website has been created by me from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Please feel free to look at my portfolio and learn more about me!

About Me

I belong to a family of five. I have a twin sister Claire, and a younger sister Sophie, both of whom also are students at BYU. I have lived in Heber City, UT for the majority of my life, growing up loving the outdoors. I love to travel with my family and spend time with them when I'm not busy with school.

One of my favorite hobbies is playing the bagpipes. Beginning with my grandma immigrating to the United States from Scotland and starting a family of her own in Utah, I am proud of my Scottish heritage. Starting at 11 years old, I learned how to play the bagpipes from my dad. We both are currently members of the Salt Lake Scots Pipe Band, serving others in the community with our musical abilities. I have entered multiple competitions in Utah, both competing solo and with the band. I love continuing to grow my talents and look forward to further developing this skill.

During the winter I love to snow ski with my family. For the majority of my life my family has made trips almost weekly every winter to Park City to go skiing. I love spending time in the mountains, especially with my family.

For two years I had the opportunity to serve a full-time proselyting mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in the greater St. Louis, MO area. This experience changed my life and taught me the importance of charity and kindness. My service not only helped others overcome struggles in their personal lives, but it also helped me change the way I view and treat other people.
